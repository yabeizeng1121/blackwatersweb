Our Approach

What is saltwater intrusion?

Saltwater intrusion occurs when saltwater from the ocean moves upstream into freshwater rivers, which can be caused by a number of different factors, many of which are linked to climate change, and it has significant impacts on coastal ecosystems.

Causes of Saltwater Intrusion

SEA LEVEL RISE

Sea water can encroach on coastal ecosystems

STORMS AND TIDES

Can bring saltwater further inland

DROUGHT

Freshwater evaporates and allows saltwater to flow inland

WATER MANAGEMENT

Ground and surface water extraction

CONNECTIVITY

Canals, ditches, and dams

Why do we care?

Coastal forests can't tolerate high salinity, which alters soil composition and prevents seedlings from germinating. This leads to the decline of these forests, creating ghost forests.

Nutrient overloading in water bodies causes algal blooms. When these blooms die, they deplete oxygen, leading to the death of aquatic ecosystems.
