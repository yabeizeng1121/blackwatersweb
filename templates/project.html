Analyzing Coastal Blackwater Rivers

We use satellite imagery to analyze the salinity and water quality of coastal blackwater rivers, focusing on color indicators like Chlorophyll-a, suspended sediment, and colored dissolved organic matter. This builds on Dr. John Gardner's research using Landsat and Sentinel-2 data through Google Earth Engine.

Our project employs Google Earth Engine, the National Hydrology Dataset, Python, and RStudio for data analysis. We also developed an interactive R Shiny app to visualize seasonal patterns and long-term changes in these ecosystems.

Our methodology involves compiling river mouth coordinates, mapping flowlines with the NHD package in R, and analyzing relevant pixels in Google Earth Engine. We expand the area of interest and use masks to filter non-water pixels, aiming to understand changes in salinity and water quality.

Flowline Creation and Buffering

To create an accurate flowline, we generate upstream and downstream segments from a coordinate point using the NHD, stitching them together to closely follow the river, especially near its mouth.

We consider all points within a given radius around the flowline, ensuring the entire river is analyzed, not just a central line. Then remove the land pixels to return the actual flowline.
